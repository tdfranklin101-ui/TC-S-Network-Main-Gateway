Founder's Note

April 10, 2025

Dear Supporters, Innovators, and Fellow Planetary Citizens,

Today marks a significant moment in our shared journey as we launch The Current-See—a bold experiment in recognizing and distributing the abundant value of our sun.

For too long, we've operated within economic systems that prioritize scarcity and extraction. The Current-See offers a different path—one based on natural abundance, transparent distribution, and planet-first thinking.

"The sun shines its energy upon all of us equally. Why shouldn't its value be shared the same way?"

Since April 7, 2025, when we activated the Solar Clock, our prototype has been tracking and tokenizing a tiny fraction (just 1%) of the daily solar energy hitting Earth. This energy—all 174 petawatts of it—exists whether we acknowledge it or not. But by recognizing its value and creating a transparent system for its accounting and distribution, we open new possibilities for what an economy can be.

The Current-See is not just a technological innovation—it's a fundamental reimagining of value. The Solar, our energy-denominated asset, is neither mined nor extracted. It requires no complicated consensus mechanisms or energy-intensive validations. It simply recognizes what already exists: the life-sustaining energy that powers our entire planet.

As we move forward with our Solar Generator prototype, we invite you to participate, question, and contribute. This is an open system designed to evolve with your input. Sign up to secure your daily solar allocation, track its growth, and help us refine this model for global implementation.

The sun rises each day without discrimination. Its energy falls on every square meter of our planet, regardless of the political borders, economic systems, or social structures we've created. The Current-See aims to mirror this natural equality in the distribution of value.

Thank you for joining us on this journey. Together, we're building more than just a prototype—we're creating a blueprint for a more equitable, sustainable economic future.

With gratitude and optimism,

Terry D. Franklin

Founder, The Current-See PBC, Inc.
