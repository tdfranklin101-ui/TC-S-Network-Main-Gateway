Multilingual Voice Assistant

Our AI-powered voice assistant helps you navigate The Current-See ecosystem, providing information about solar energy, conservation tips, and personalized guidance on using your SOLAR tokens.

The assistant is designed to be accessible in multiple languages and can be activated with a simple click or voice command.

How to Try It:

  1. Look for the circular microphone button in the bottom-right corner of any page
  2. Click the button to open the voice assistant panel
  3. Try asking questions like:
    • "How does The Current-See work?"
    • "Give me energy conservation tips"
    • "What are the benefits of solar energy?"
    • "How do I check my SOLAR balance?"
    • "Tell me about solar tracking"
  4. Use either voice input (click the microphone button inside the panel) or type your questions

The voice assistant uses the Web Speech API for voice recognition and is compatible with most modern browsers. If your browser doesn't support speech recognition, you can still type your questions.

One-Click Language Translation

The Current-See is designed to be accessible globally. Our instant translation feature allows visitors to view the entire website in their preferred language with a single click.

The language selector is located in the top-right corner of every page and provides translation to over 10 common languages.

How to Try It:

  1. Locate the language selector dropdown in the header (looks like a globe or flag icon)
  2. Click to open the dropdown menu
  3. Select your desired language from the list
  4. Watch as the page content is instantly translated
  5. To return to English, select the English (US) option from the dropdown

Translation is powered by Google Translate and may not be perfect for technical terms or complex sentences. The voice assistant will attempt to respond in your selected language when possible.

Feature Integration

The voice assistant and language translation features work together seamlessly. When you change the language using the language selector, the voice assistant will attempt to recognize and respond in that language as well.

This integration creates a truly multilingual experience that makes The Current-See accessible to users around the world, regardless of their native language.

Speech recognition quality may vary by language. Some languages may have better recognition accuracy than others, depending on your browser and the Web Speech API implementation.
