  <h1>The Genius Act — Toward Ethical, Transparent & Human-Centered Intelligence</h1>
  <p class="note"><strong>Working Theory Draft v0.3 — October 2025</strong><br>
  Companion to the <a href="/uim-whitepaper.html">UIM White Paper</a> • TC-S Network Foundation Research Publication</p>

  <div class="divider"></div>

  <h2>1 · Executive Summary</h2>
  <p>The Genius Act outlines a moral and technical framework to ensure AI-augmented systems and energy-denominated economies uphold dignity, privacy, and ecological balance. It extends the UIM white paper from identity and wallet design to governance and compliance theory.</p>

  <h2>2 · Scope and Intent</h2>
  <p>This document is an evolving research theory — not a statute or final policy. It serves as the Foundation’s discussion draft for how public-benefit AI and energy markets should operate ethically across the TC-S ecosystem.</p>

  <h2>3 · Core Principles</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Data Dignity & Privacy →</strong> Minimize collection and store locally whenever possible.</li>
    <li><strong>Energy Accountability →</strong> Every transaction and model cycle tied to measurable kWh inputs.</li>
    <li><strong>Transparency →</strong> All pricing and governance formulas published for audit.</li>
    <li><strong>Human Oversight →</strong> Humans approve policy changes and AI model updates.</li>
    <li><strong>Market Integrity →</strong> Research-mode markets only until compliance certification is finalized.</li>
    <li><strong>Environmental Responsibility →</strong> Renewable source disclosure and life-cycle accounting.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>4 · Relation to UIM Framework</h2>
  <p>Where UIM defines identity and wallet architecture, the Genius Act defines how those systems behave ethically under audit and oversight. Together they form the technical and moral spine of the Foundation’s operations.</p>

  <h2>5 · Implementation Path (Research Roadmap)</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>2026 Q1 – Q2 · Expert review and community comment period.</li>
    <li>2026 Q3 · Public discussion and draft revision.</li>
    <li>2027 · Inclusion in Foundation bylaws and compliance tooling.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>6 · Legal Context</h2>
  <p>The Genius Act does not supersede law. It frames how the Foundation interprets and applies emerging AI and digital-asset regulations within a public-benefit model.</p>

  <h2>7 · Living Document & Feedback</h2>
  <p>Scholars, regulators, and citizens are invited to review and comment. Feedback channels will open after peer review is complete. No sign-ups or solicitations are requested.</p>
