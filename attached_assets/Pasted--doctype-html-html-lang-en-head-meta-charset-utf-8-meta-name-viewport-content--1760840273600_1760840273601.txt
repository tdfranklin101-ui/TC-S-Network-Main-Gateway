      <section class="panel">
        <h2>Genius Act Compliance — <span class="wip">Working Theory</span></h2>
        <p class="note">
          The Foundation is developing an internal compliance framework referred to as the <em>“Genius Act”</em> to guide responsible
          research and public-benefit operations. The points below are <strong>draft principles</strong> under community/legal review.
          They describe intent, not finalized policy or legal advice.
        </p>

        <details>
          <summary>1) Data Dignity & Privacy</summary>
          <ul class="list">
            <li>User data minimization; no sale of personal data.</li>
            <li>Clear purpose limitation for telemetry and analytics; opt-out where feasible.</li>
            <li>Default non-custodial posture for wallets; user retains control of keys where applicable.</li>
          </ul>
        </details>

        <details>
          <summary>2) Energy-Backed Accounting (kWh→Solar)</summary>
          <ul class="list">
            <li>Transparent price equations linking artifacts to energy footprint (kWh) and Solar unit equivalence.</li>
            <li>Ledger entries include provenance, timestamp, and conversion factors for auditability.</li>
            <li>Retire-on-redemption model for energy delivery claims (where PPAs/RECs integrate in future phases).</li>
          </ul>
        </details>

        <details>
          <summary>3) Safety, Human Oversight & Model Governance</summary>
          <ul class="list">
            <li>Human-in-the-loop review for material changes, model updates, and policy shifts.</li>
            <li>Documented model sources, assumptions, and limitations for any AI-assisted pricing or ranking.</li>
            <li>Incident response runbooks and rollback capabilities for erroneous listings or balances.</li>
          </ul>
        </details>

        <details>
          <summary>4) Market Integrity (Research Mode)</summary>
          <ul class="list">
            <li>No promises of financial return or tradability; prototype is for demonstration and feedback.</li>
            <li>Anti-fraud checks on artifact metadata; clear “prototype” labeling on all user-visible flows.</li>
            <li>Public changelog for breaking changes and deprecations.</li>
          </ul>
        </details>

        <details>
          <summary>5) Legal & Regulatory Orientation</summary>
          <ul class="list">
            <li>Jurisdictional scan for consumer protection, digital assets, and energy credit interfaces.</li>
            <li>AML/KYC readiness designs for any future fiat on-/off-ramps (not active in the prototype).</li>
            <li>Foundation vs. PBC role separation and arm’s-length assistance to preserve public-benefit mandate.</li>
          </ul>
        </details>

        <details>
          <summary>6) Environmental Reporting</summary>
          <ul class="list">
            <li>Disclosure of energy accounting assumptions and generator sources when available.</li>
            <li>Plans for third-party verification of renewable claims in future phases.</li>
          </ul>
        </details>

        <div class="sep"></div>
        <p class="note">

          certification, or compliance attestation. Final documentation will be published after expert review.
        </p>
